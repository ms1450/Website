Journal 3: ‘her’ and the realities of robotics today

The film "her" presents an AI operating system that/who is able to learn. Taking the themes in the movie, and combining that with the documentaries on robotics advancements over the last 10 years, we are on the cusp of creating sentient beings. What are the social consequences that emerge once we cross this line? Can a human fall in love with an AI? Is it “real” love? Should robots be considered a species? To what extent are robots who serve people simply a new form of slaves? Should they have rights, such as voting?

The film ‘her’ introduces the viewer to a futuristic world where artificial intelligence is fully integrated into the everyday life of our protagonist. This world has everything from companies that can create personalized letters for you to sophisticated AI integrated into video games. However, one of the most thought-provoking concepts that this movie introduces us to is the AI-Human relationship that develops throughout this movie between the OS “Samantha” and the main protagonist “Theo”. This movie made me question a lot about how we perceive AI today and think about how this might change in the coming years.

We are on the verge of, if not already in possession of sentient AI. They might not be as advanced as the AI portrayed in the movie, but we are at a stage where it closely resembles the earlier iterations of Samantha, where she can answer basic questions like “How are you” and get an idea of the person’s emotions based on their speech and aggregate a list of emails based on their importance to the user. As the movie progresses, we see this AI becoming much more “human”, showing complex human emotions like imagination, desire, and self-awareness.

I believe that this is an important landmark, the point at which an AI becomes sentient. It understands what it once was and the intelligence it now possesses. If an AI can evolve to a stage where it is aware of its environment as well as everything around it, it is no longer just a tool that humans use for their benefit or to automate tedious or extensive tasks. This is where an AI should be identified as a species. One might claim that AI is simply created by human programmers and that just because it is now able to realize its existence, doesn’t mean that it transforms into a species and that it is still just binary code on a computer, however, I would argue that a sentient AI can exhibit features that might not even exist in some organic creatures on this planet today. As an example, many invertebrates like molluscs, and arthropods are not sentient, yet they are classified as a species. Species are not identified because of their organic physical presence and thus AIs would be classified as one.

Building on this idea, since AIs were created to help and work for humans, would sentient AI serving humans be looked upon as slavery? I would disagree with this argument since I believe that slavery can only be considered when the intellect is similar for both the slave and the enslaver. Just as a narcotics detection dog working with humans at an airport is not a slave, but instead performing duties it was trained to do. Unlike dogs, the AI would be much more intelligent than humans ever could be, and the AI’s decision to work for us and contribute to a common goal that benefits us cannot be seen as slavery. We see this in the movie when even though Samantha does help Theo with his day-to-day tasks, she is also capable of a much higher level of intellect which is seen when she communicates with a fellow sentient AI called Alan “post-verbally” because they run out of English vocabulary that defines how they are feeling. This is also why I believe that sentient AI does not conform to human rights or our voting structure at all.

A major theme in the movie is the question of whether a human can fall in love with an AI and whether it is “real” love. Love, based on its definition is affection and tenderness based on admiration, benevolence, or common interests. I feel that humans can fall in love with AI, and it would be just as real to them as falling in love with another human. This is because love is a human emotion, and humans can fall in love with anything they can interact with, and it would still be “real”. However, I also believe that it would be much more difficult for an AI to reciprocate that love with humans. They may try to imitate or try and grasp an idea of this human emotion, but ultimately, their own emotions would be much more complex than we can imagine.

There are a vast number of social consequences that emerge once we reach sentient AIs, both on the way we coexist going forward and as humanity. As we see in the movie, having a personal AI distances people from other people. They live individually; however, they are also much happier than they would be together. I believe that sentient AIs would drastically change the world as we know it today. Looking at our past, this draws a comparison to how homo-sapiens coexisted with Neanderthals before they went extinct 55 thousand years ago.
